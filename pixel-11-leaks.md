# A New Era for Android Hardware
The tech world is currently buzzing with massive structural leaks regarding Google's next flagship smartphone catalog: **The Pixel 11 lineup**. 

Historically, Google has struggled to match the raw thermal efficiency and modem connectivity of Qualcomm and Apple. However, the Pixel 11 represents a drastic shift in supply-chain strategy that could finally level the playing field. Here is everything we know leading up to the expected **August 2026** launch.

## 1. The Power of Tensor G6
The most significant upgrade lies beneath the glass. Google is heavily rumored to be utilizing a custom-built **Tensor G6 processor** manufactured on a cutting-edge 2nm process. 

This 7-core architecture is designed explicitly to accelerate on-device Machine Learning (specifically Gemini Nano), dramatically lowering battery consumption while performing heavy AI computation. 

## 2. Goodbye Samsung Modems
For years, Pixel users have endured connectivity drops and severe battery drain caused by older Samsung Exynos modems. Leaks indicate that Google is finally moving on, sourcing a **MediaTek M90 5G modem** for the entire Pixel 11 family. This structural shift is expected to radically improve 5G reliability and thermal efficiency.

## 3. The "Bear" Codenames
Google has apparently adopted bear-themed internal codenames for the upcoming devices, and the lineup is massive:
* **Pixel 11** — *"Cubs"* 
* **Pixel 11 Pro** — *"Grizzly"*
* **Pixel 11 Pro XL** — *"Kodiak"*
* **Pixel 11 Pro Fold** — *"Yogi"*

The pricing is expected to remain consistent with last year, keeping the standard "Cubs" model firmly in the highly competitive **$799** bracket. 

## 4. Hardware Refinements
The physical design language isn't getting overhauled, but it's receiving the premium polish it needed:
* **Razor-Thin Bezels:** CAD renders show significantly shrunken bezels across the entire lineup.
* **Camera Visor:** Expect a newly tweaked, edge-to-edge black camera bar design.
* **Longevity Specs:** Base models are finally shipping with a massive **12GB of RAM**, a **5,000mAh battery**, and native **Qi2 Magnetic Charging** right out of the box.

If these leaks hold true, the Pixel 11 will be the most complete, technologically flawless device Google has ever shipped.
